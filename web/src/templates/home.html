Key Value Proposition

Key Value Proposition

Our plant monitoring system, PlantPal, helps amateur gardeners who wish to learn proper plant maintenance for healthier plants by displaying soil moisture, sunlight, and temperature history on our app and sending reminders and tips for plant maintenance. Unlike passive maintenance systems such as automated sprinklers, our product actively reads plant data to provide more optimate caretaking. Additionally, our product acts as a learning tool for amateur hobbyists who want to learn more about gardening

Product Page

Product

PlantPal is a network of devices to monitor various factors such as soil moisture, sunlight levels, and temperature to provide optimal instructions for plant maintenance. PlantPal works by taking readings from capacitive PCB, a photoresistor, and a thermistor. The capacitive PCB will stick into the soil of a plant that needs monitoring and will read voltage outputs from a pulse. Depending on the moisture content of the soil, the capacitance in the circuit changes which will distort the pulse. From these distortions, we can extrapolate the moisture content of the soil. Similarly, we will use readings from a photoresistor and a thermistor to read sunlight levels and temperature. Our product will record the history of these variables and present them graphically on our UI. Based on these readings, our product will provide instructions via reminders through our app for plant maintenance such as watering, draining, or moving locations to optimize healthy plant growth.

Customer

This product is designed for amateur hobbyists who want to get into gardening. Nowadays plants are imported from all parts of the world. Each plant thrives in different conditions and as a novice gardener, it is difficult to determine the optimate conditions for each plant to prevent them from dying. Our product acts as a trainer by providing reminders, tips, and instructions for plant maintenance for beautiful, healthy plants.

Key Benefits

A key benefit of our product is active monitoring. Because our product is constantly reading plant growth variables we're able to provide optimal instruction for plant maintenance resulting in a healthier plant.

Features

1. Centralized Smartphone Hub

The biggest feature of our product is the Smartphone App. It keeps track of plant data over time, presents it to the user in an easy-to-understand format, and provides tips and reminders for plant health. The main benefit of this feature is that it teaches and trains users to build good plant taking habits and introduces them to gardening. Novice gardeners will benefit the most from this feature as it teaches them to be a better gardener. Hobbyist can also benefit from feature as it makes it easier to monitor thier plant's health.

2. Capacitive Moisture Detection

Our product will detect the moisture levels of the plant with this feature. From our second round user assessment we saw that 4/11 responses stated some sort of issue with watering their plants when asked if they had experienced any problesm taking care of plants. Over/underwatering often leads to plants dying so this is very important. The main benefit of this feature is that it informs the users if their plant has enough water and when they need to water it which reduces the odds of accidently killing the plant. Novice gardeners will benefit most from this feature as it will tell the user when/when not to water their plants.

3. Sunlight Level Reader

Our product will detect the sunlight levels the plant receives. Various plants have different needs for sunlight, too much/too little sunlight can easily kill healthy plants. The main benefit of this feature is that the tells the user the ideal placement of their plants for healthy growth reducing chances of providing too much/too little sunlight. Once again novice gardeners will benefit the most from this feature as it tells them where to place their plants for best growth.

4. Temperature Sensor

Our product will measure temperature over time in the soil and on the surface. Like the previous features, plant growth can be impacted by temperature. The man benefit of this feature is that it will notify the user when the plants are in suboptimal temperatures which can inhibit proper growth.

5. Interchangeable Tops

Our product has an optional flag on top that users can customize. The main benefits of this feature is that it labels the various plants to avoid and adds a decorative element to the product. This mostly benefits those with multiple plants as it can help avoid confusion between plants especialy in early stages when there are not distictive features between plants. This reduces human error in providing care to the wrong plants.

Interactions

Sensor Installation

The first thing anyone has to do when they purchase a new product is set it up. For our product this means placing the sensor properly into the soil. One visual cue for our product is the shape of it. It has T shape with a pointed bottom. This visual cue implies that the pointed section goes into the soil and the top fo the T acts as a hard stop for the product so it doesn't go too deep into the soil. The shape of the product helps with error reduction with inproper installation.

Health Tracking

The app is mean to help teach gardening skills to beginers. Because of this it is very important for the UI to be intutive so that the user is not confused. With our home page having visuals for the plants it helps the user know which plant data they are looking at. When they tap on the plant it provides more specific data and graphs. This helps with efficiency, learnabilty, and memorability.

Law of UI Design Examples

Hick's Law

My Toaster oven is an example of Hick's Law. Desision making increase with the number/complexity of options. I only use it to head up croissants from costco but I don't really know what all the knobs do except for the temperature. From my experimentation the options in the middle option kind of do the same thing when heading up a croissant. The bottom know switches between pictures of toast to time which doesn't really make sense. The pictures of toast don't even correspond to levels of toast. The half toast icon burns the toast the full toast icon will turn it completely black. The interface for this toaster is too complex and the possible options are confusing. I just want to toast some croissants.

Law of Proximity

My keyboard is an example of the law of proximity. It groups the different buttons on the keyboard into section for different uses. The main section has all the letter keys intended for typing. The arrow keys are seperate and intended for navigation. The numpad is also seperate and grouped for mathematical applications. I think the design for the keyboard which groups various buttons in different sections helps organize information for a better UI.

Doherty Threshold

The remote to turn on my celing fan is an example of the doherty treshold. This remote has a very large delay, I have to hold the button for ~2 seconds for any settings on the fan to update. It is extremely annoying to use and as a result I don't use it. Because of this laggy remote I lose attention and feel like the product is not very productive despite functioning properly.

Week 6

Product Information Architecture

1. The typical user needs access to the status of the plant in regards to various environmental factors. This includes sunlight level, water moisture, and temperature. The home page will display qualitative statuses like great, good, poor, and bad. When tap on the plant it will provide more detailed quantitative data like the specific temperature or moisture percentage.

2. Principle of disclosure is important for our product. We don't want to overwelm the user with a bunch of data and charts. We will present a top layer that is very simplied to show overall health and will go more into specifics as they dive deeper. The principle of exemplars is also important for our product. Our product is advertised as an educational tool for those who want to get into gardening. Because of this the IA should behave in a consistent manner to not confuse costumers as they are learning a new skill.

3.I would imagine that a mix of hierarchical and topic would make sense. A hierarchy could be useful to show the plants in most dire need of attention. If a plant needs to be watered we could push it up on the interface so the user knows that particular plant needs attention. We could also classify the plants in our app by types. For example putting fruits with fruits, and succulents with succulents.

Visual Hierarchy

Color and Contrast

On my website I use color and contrast to highlight interative features to help guide users. For example when the mouse hovers over the menu items it switches from a dull green to a very dark green. The dark green contrast the cream colored background and indicates that menu buttons perform an important task. I use this same contrast to differentiate between the weekly updates. THe previous weeks are greyed out to indicate that they are not the most recent update while the current update is a dark green to show that it is the active update.

Typographical Hierarchy

The sizing of text is also important on my website. The larger heading text indicate a significant section of the webite. Under each header is smaller subsections which are a medium font size and the paragraphs which are smaller. The sizing of the typology help emphasize which words are most important.

Spacing

Spacing helps split my webpage into sections. Each section has a divider which is mapped to the menu buttons. The space and line in between helps seperate the sections so it is clear which section of th website users are on
